# Hook & Structure Library

Raw material for fast drafting. These are *patterns*, not fill-in-the-blank clichés — adapt the
mechanism to the specific topic, audience, and voice. Each pattern notes the mechanism it
exploits (cross-ref `research-foundations.md`).

## 1. Verbal hook patterns (first 0–1.5s)
- **Contrarian correction** — "Everything you've been told about X is wrong." (gap + arousal)
- **Specific-gap question** — "Why does [specific thing] always [specific outcome]?" (small gap)
- **Stakes up front** — "I spent [resource] so you don't have to." (curiosity + utility)
- **Mid-action open** — drop in as if the conversation already started: "…and that's the part
  nobody tells you." (kills the intro, implies you missed something)
- **Numbered promise** — "Three things that [outcome] in [timeframe]." (closed, scannable loop)
- **Pattern-break confession** — "I was doing this completely wrong for two years." (relatable
  + gap)
- **Forbidden frame** — "I probably shouldn't show you this." (mild taboo → orienting response)
> Never: "Hey guys, welcome back, in today's video…" — retention cliff.

## 2. On-screen title/text hook patterns
- Title hook (3–6 words, top third): the *stakes* in plain words — "THE HOOK MISTAKE."
- Text hook (under chin): **contradict** the verbal line for tension — voice says "easy," text
  says "almost nobody gets this right."
- Keyword reveal: surface only the load-bearing word per beat, bolded/colored (signaling).

## 3. Pattern-interrupt menu (rotate — don't repeat the same type)
Visual: jump cut · angle change · push-in/zoom · B-roll insert · color flash.
Auditory: SFX punch · music drop to silence · cadence change · sudden volume shift.
Kinetic: stand/sit · pick up prop · walk into/out of frame · gesture · turn to new camera.
Textual: new caption appears · emoji animation · number on screen.
Semantic: a contradiction · a rhetorical question · a surprising statistic.
> Mechanism: each re-fires the orienting response, which habituates within seconds (§2).

## 4. Short-form body structures
- **Problem → agitate → reveal** — name pain, twist the knife, deliver the fix at the end.
- **List with a withheld best** — "#3 is the one that actually matters" (open loop to the end).
- **Myth → bust → proof** — claim, overturn, show evidence (arousal via surprise).
- **Before/after with hidden method** — show transformation, withhold "how" until the payoff.
- **Loop sandwich** — first frame = last frame, seamless restart (watch-time multiplier).

## 5. Long-form structures
- **Stair-step escalation** — each segment raises stakes/spectacle beyond the last (RPE engine).
- **Question chain** — resolve loop A while opening B; B→C→…; macro-loop closes at the end.
- **Crazy-progression cold open** — flash later peaks (day 1→7), reset to start, then build.
- **Story circle (Harmon/Campbell, 8 beats)** — you/need/go/search/find/take/return/changed.
  Best for narrative/documentary and serialized content (maximizes neural coupling).
- **Re-engagement markers** — write explicit high-stimulation beats at ~3 min and ~6 min.

## 6. Title formulas (YouTube — small specific gap, no spoiler)
- "I [did extreme thing] for [timeframe]" — stakes + curiosity.
- "Why [surprising subject] is [counterintuitive claim]" — gap.
- "The [superlative] [thing] (and what it taught me)" — implied payoff.
- "[Number] [things] that [outcome]" — closed loop, scannable.
- "What happens when [unusual setup]?" — open question.
> CTR and AVD must rise together — if the title overpromises the payoff, it's punished.

## 7. Thumbnail concept formulas
- **Face + strong emotion** (surprise/shock/intense focus), eyes visible, ≤4 words text.
- **Contrast pair** — complementary high-contrast colors; subject pops off background.
- **Before/after split** — implies transformation, opens the gap.
- **Object of desire/horror centered** — the thing the title promises, partially revealed.
- **The "wait, what?" juxtaposition** — two things that shouldn't be together.
> Thumbnail + title should pose a question the *video* answers — never spoil the payoff.

## 8. CTA / ending patterns by goal metric
- **Sends (Instagram)** — make the payoff so specific the viewer pictures one friend; optional
  soft prompt "send this to the person who needs it." Specificity > broad reach.
- **Completion / loop (TikTok)** — loop close (last frame = first frame); no outro.
- **Saves** — "save this for when you…" tied to a concrete future moment.
- **Comments** — end on a genuine open question or a mild, defensible take that invites reply.
- **Watch-time / session (YouTube)** — abrupt close on the payoff + end-screen into a
  **playlist** led by your best-retaining videos.
- **Click-through (ads/funnels)** — single clear action, one verb, frictionless next step.
> Universal rule: end ON the payoff. Padding and "thanks for watching" sag the curve.
