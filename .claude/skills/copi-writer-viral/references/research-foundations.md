## 1. The attention-span myth vs. the real bottleneck
The "8-second attention span, shorter than a goldfish" stat (Microsoft Canada 2015, sourced
to the defunct "Statistic Brain") was debunked by the BBC (2017) and Fast Company (2024); the
goldfish comparator was invented. **Real finding:** Gloria Mark (UC Irvine, *Attention Span*,
2023) measured average attention on a single computer screen falling from ~2.5 min (2004) to
~47 sec (since ~2016) — but that's *task-switching frequency in multitasking*, NOT a sustained-
attention ceiling. Humans still watch hours of video when held. **Operational truth:** the
bottleneck is the near-zero cost of swiping away, not a shrunken brain. You're fighting exit
cost, not attention capacity.

## 2. Orienting response & pattern interrupts
The **orienting response** (Sokolov, 1963) is the reflex that aims the senses at novelty
(heart-rate dips, skin conductance rises). It **habituates fast** — as the brain models the
stimulus, the response fades (the Novelty-P3 ERP and skin-conductance amplitude decline
near-exponentially with repetition and recover when something novel appears). A **pattern
interrupt** (cut, angle change, prop, SFX, physical action) is a stimulus discrepant from the
just-built model, so it re-fires the orienting response. This is the mechanism behind "reset
attention every 2–4 seconds." Attention is best modeled as a **priority map** integrating
salience, goals, reward history, and novelty (Theeuwes; Gonthier 2026) — faces, motion, sudden
onsets, and high contrast all capture it bottom-up even when irrelevant.

## 3. Curiosity gaps & open loops
**Information Gap Theory** (Loewenstein, 1994, *Psych Bulletin*): curiosity fires when attention
focuses on a *gap* in one's knowledge. Key nuance — **small, specific gaps produce the most
curiosity** (they imply you almost know); huge vague gaps suppress it. fMRI links curiosity to
reward-anticipation regions (Kang et al., 2009). The **Zeigarnik effect** (1927): unfinished
tasks stay active in memory — the basis of the open loop. **Caveat:** too many simultaneous
loops cause overload/abandonment; micro-loops (resolved in seconds) outperform one giant
cliffhanger. This is why §3 says "small, specific gap" and "micro-loop every 5–8s."

## 4. Reward prediction error (RPE)
Dopamine neurons fire on the *difference* between predicted and actual reward, not reward
itself (Schultz, 1997/2016). Better-than-expected → dopamine spike; as-expected → nothing;
variable/unpredictable schedules maximize response. **Translation:** a telegraphed payoff
produces no spike; a payoff that *exceeds* the viewer's implicit prediction (bigger reveal,
sharper twist, faster joke) reinforces watching. This grounds "stair-step escalation" and
"don't telegraph the answer." (Also the basis of the "TikTok brain" compulsive-use literature
— still maturing, mostly cross-sectional; cite cautiously.)

## 5. Arousal & sharing
Berger & Milkman, "What Makes Online Content Viral?" (*J. Marketing Research*, 2012; ~2,800
citations): **physiological arousal drives sharing, not positivity.** High-arousal awe (+),
anger (+), anxiety (+) all boost virality; low-arousal sadness and contentment suppress it.
"Practically useful" content was an independent positive predictor — hence **awe + utility >
pure spectacle.** Replicated for images (Deza & Parikh 2015; Gelli et al. 2015).

## 6. Platform signals — SOLID vs. FOLKLORE
**Solid (officially stated by platforms):**
- Instagram (Mosseri, 2023–2024): top Reels signals are **sends per reach**, watch-time, likes;
  "sends per reach" repeatedly named the strongest reach signal. ~half of Reels watched muted.
- TikTok (2020 transparency post): watch-to-completion, rewatches, shares, comments, likes,
  follows are interest indicators (no public weights).
- YouTube: CTR, average view duration, and session watch-time dominate; relative retention
  decides recommendations; playlists extend session time.
- Meta: "3-second video play" and "2-second continuous play" are defined view metrics.

**Folklore (vendor/creator estimates, NOT peer-reviewed — directional only):**
- "8.4s average TikTok watch time," "70–85% completion needed for virality," "captions raise
  completion 32%," "71% decide in 3 seconds," "1.7s of mobile attention on Facebook." Use as
  rhetoric, not evidence. Specific weights also drift year to year.

**MrBeast leaked production doc (2024):** verified authentic by Business Insider; it's
creator-practice, not research. Its tenets — CTR/AVD/AVP focus, decisive first minute, "crazy
progression," re-engagements ~3 & ~6 min, stair-stepping, abrupt endings — align neatly with
§§2–5 above, which is *suggestive*, not validating.

## 7. Cognitive load & captions
Sweller's **Cognitive Load Theory** + Mayer's **Multimedia Learning** principles:
- **Coherence** — cut decoration/B-roll/music that doesn't carry meaning (it's wasted load).
- **Signaling** — highlight the essential (bold keyword, arrow) → better outcomes.
- **Modality** — narration + image beats on-screen-text + image for complex material.
- **Redundancy** — identical full text + identical audio *hurts* complex learning.
**Resolution of the caption paradox:** on muted social feeds, captions aren't redundant —
they're the primary channel for half the audience. So: short **keyword** captions (signaling),
segmented word-by-word (Kruger et al. 2013 — faster reading, better comprehension), NOT full
transcripts. Dual-coding (Paivio 1971) + Picture Superiority (Paivio & Csapo 1973 — images
remembered 2×+ words) is why "convert claims to visuals."

## 8. Narrative & neural coupling
Green & Brock **Transportation Imagery Model** (2000): absorbed viewers counter-argue less and
shift beliefs more. Stephens, Silbert & Hasson (PNAS 2010): a listener's brain activity
**aligns with the speaker's** during natural narration, with anticipatory coupling when
communication succeeds — and the coupling *vanishes* when it fails. **Implication:** story is
the strongest brain-sync mechanism we have; bullet-point video doesn't couple, narrative does.
Gimmicks without payoff break the coupling.

## 9. Pacing history
Cutting et al., "Quicker, faster, darker" (2011): average shot length in Hollywood film fell
logarithmically from ~12s (1930s) to ~2.5s (modern), with rising within-shot visual activity.
Modern high-retention YouTube pushes ~1.5–2.5s in opening minutes. The trend reflects editing
technology + priority-map dynamics, **not** a literal attention-span collapse — don't conflate.

## 10. Multisensory integration (superadditivity)
Stein & Meredith (*The Merging of the Senses*, 1993): a neuron's response to a paired audio+visual
event can *exceed the sum* of its responses to each alone — **superadditivity**. Two rules follow.
**Temporal coincidence:** the boost only happens inside the **audiovisual temporal binding window
(~100–250ms)**; outside it the events decouple — so an SFX 300ms off its cut does nothing.
**Inverse effectiveness** (Stanford & Stein 2007): the *weaker* each element, the *larger* the
proportional gain from pairing — tiny coincident events (a 2-frame text pop + a faint tick) capture
attention disproportionately. This is the science behind the "100ms rule" and stacking micro-events
on beats. Sound also pre-primes vision: an involuntary orienting cue (a whoosh) desynchronizes
occipital alpha and *improves* processing of the visual that follows (Störmer/McDonald).

## 11. Beat entrainment (Nozaradan, Large, Lenc)
Nozaradan, Peretz & Mouraux (J. Neurosci. 2012): the auditory cortex **entrains** to a musical beat —
cortical oscillations lock to the beat frequency. Per Large's Dynamic Attending Theory, attention is
**heightened at expected beat times**. So on-beat cuts land at attentional peaks and off-beat cuts in
troughs — but cutting *every* beat overloads (coherence violation): cut on downbeats/half-bar accents.
Lenc et al. (PNAS 2018): **low-frequency sounds strengthen beat tracking** — bass-heavy beds make
beat-matched cuts feel more satisfying. (The "beat-sync = 2.3× engagement" stat is vendor folklore;
the mechanism is solid, the multiplier isn't.)

## 12. Music, dopamine & frisson (Salimpoor & Zatorre; Huron)
Salimpoor, Zatorre et al. (Nature Neuroscience 2011, PET+fMRI): music releases striatal dopamine, and
**anticipation and peak use anatomically distinct pathways** (caudate during the build, nucleus
accumbens at the peak) — meaning *the build is itself rewarding*, so earn the drop. Huron
(*Sweet Anticipation*, 2006) names the acoustic correlates of "chills": a sudden large loudness
increase (subito forte), broadening of the frequency range (added sub-bass and/or high treble), and
the entry of a new voice/instrument. That's the literal recipe for the "this changes everything"
reveal stack. (Frisson reliably fires in only ~30–50% of people, but the same features produce
sub-threshold arousal in most listeners.)

## 13. Voice, prosody & speech rate
**Coupling (extends §8):** Stephens, Silbert & Hasson (PNAS 2010) is a *voice* finding too — flat,
monotone, or uncanny delivery breaks the speaker→listener neural sync, so pitch and loudness variation
aren't theatrics, they're the coupling mechanism. Current synthetic/cloned voices still partially break
it (uncanny micro-prosody) — prefer human voice for high-stakes teaching. **Modality (Mayer):**
narration over visuals beats on-screen-text over visuals for complex material (median *d* ≈ 0.88 across
35 experiments, Mayer 2017) — voice is the carrier channel for dense AI/tech content. **Speech rate:**
Foulke & Sticht (Psych. Bulletin 1969) put the comprehension cliff at ~275 WPM; the physiological
speaking ceiling is ~215 WPM (so faster on screen = post-production time-compression). Operational
window: ~155 WPM raw → jump-cut to 180–200 (tutorial) or 200–230 (news/hype). **Prosody:** wider pitch
range and loudness variation signal positive activation (Juslin & Laukka 2003; Larrouy-Maestri et al.
2025); a pause *before* a key term is a signaling cue that aids encoding.

## Folklore flags (audio-specific)
Treat as directional, not proven: "beat-synced = 2.3× engagement" (self-referential vendor blog);
"trending sounds = 2–5× reach" (untraceable — clustering *mechanism* is real, multiplier isn't);
exact BPM-to-genre tables (craft layered on psychophysics); specific trending-sound lifecycle
day-windows (algorithm-observed, not platform-published); specific platform LUFS targets (shift over
time — verify and dual-master).
