## What a Viral Pattern captures (the Q6 resolution)

**Form vs content:** capture **BOTH**, but tag each tactic's transferability so the system knows what survives a subject swap. Form/craft is the **reusable spine**; content/angle is a **thinner, explicitly-flagged layer** of subject-bound choices plus the *bridge variables* (target emotion, payoff/send-moment, genre, funnel position) that translate a subject into form decisions. A pure form-only Pattern would be unusable — the single most important authoring decision ("name the ONE moment a viewer would screenshot/send, design backward from it") is a content decision that anchors every form choice.

**Blended or separable:** **SEPARABLE, but linked.** The same form tactic fires across totally different subjects (only possible if form is factored out from content), so form is a distinct subject-independent layer. But content and form are not independent either: target emotion, send-trigger, audio emotion-vector, and funnel position are chosen *from* the content angle and then *drive* form decisions. So the model is **two separable facets with a directed dependency** — `content_angle` constrains `form_craft` *via* the `psychology_substrate` — not one blended blob, and not two orthogonal layers. Content facets carry a **rebind-on-Recreation** expectation; form facets are the durable core.

## Facet categories

- **form_craft** — how the video is built; transfers across subjects (the durable spine).
- **content_angle** — what it's about / topical strategy + funnel position; abstract moves transfer as strategies, concrete bindings rebind per subject.
- **psychology_substrate** — the cognitive/neuro mechanism (and the playback-environment constraint) explaining *why* a craft choice works; linked from the tactics it powers, not stored as a producible step.
- **platform_distribution** — algorithm signals, format/length, audio discovery, the pre-publish QA gate, the numeric iteration ladder; treat specific weights/multipliers as **directional** — plan on the mechanism.

## 1. Hook & Entry (form_craft)
First ~0–8s (+ the pre-click title/thumbnail for long-form) whose only job is to win the involuntary capture gate and open the first curiosity gap before the swipe.

- **Write the hook first / one-sentence gap test** — author the hook before anything else; if the curiosity gap can't be stated in one sentence the topic isn't ready; open mid-tension, no greeting. _Why:_ Loewenstein information-gap; a gap too diffuse to state won't fire. _Src:_ SKILL, research-foundations, cretion, audio-patterns.
- **Audience-calibrated small-specific gap** — tailor the gap to existing audience knowledge; small specific gaps fire, huge vague gaps suppress. _Why:_ Loewenstein 1994 + Kang 2009 fMRI (caudate/IFG reward-anticipation). _Src:_ SKILL, research-foundations, cretion.
- **Hook-type library (mechanism-patterns)** — rotating archetypes: contrarian correction, specific-gap question, stakes-up-front, mid-action open, numbered promise, pattern-break confession, forbidden frame, "this changes everything." Adapt the mechanism, don't fill in clichés. _Why:_ each pairs an info gap with an arousal trigger; orienting response fires on discrepancy. _Src:_ hook-structure-lib, audio-patterns.
- **Hook stack (5 channels) in first ~20s** — verbal (0–1.5s) + title overlay + text hook + pattern interrupt + re-hook at ~3–8s; span visual/auditory/kinetic/textual/semantic. _Why:_ orienting-response habituation (Sokolov 1963) — stack discrepant stimuli to re-trigger the reflex. _Src:_ SKILL, hook-structure-lib, cretion.
- **Bottom-up salience in the first frame** — face with direct gaze, motion (even a slow zoom), sudden onset, high contrast. _Why:_ priority-map salience capture (Theeuwes/Gonthier; Yantis & Jonides 1990). _Src:_ research-foundations, cretion. *(FFA-~170ms preattentive-face claim is single-sourced to cretion.)*
- **Audio bait / hook-audio curve in first second** — trending sound or "wait, what?" contradiction; hook prosody high/wide/loud/fast with a 200ms pause before the payoff word. _Why:_ sudden auditory onset triggers orienting response at the most fragile retention point. _Src:_ cretion, audio-patterns, hermes.
- **Long-form pre-click: title + thumbnail open the loop without spoiling** — title = small specific gap whose answer requires watching; thumbnail = one face/strong emotion, high contrast, ≤4 words, poses a question the title doesn't answer. _Why:_ Loewenstein gap on the pre-click decision; emotional faces + contrast lift CTR. _Src:_ SKILL, hook-structure-lib, cretion.
- **Cold-open: match thumbnail/title within ~5s, establish stakes + dominant loop, imply fast edit, no welcome.** _Why:_ RPE setup + exit-cost; survive the steep first-30s drop by honoring the click fast. _Src:_ SKILL, cretion.
- **First-line rule for text feeds** — on X/LinkedIn/static, the first line is the whole game. _Src:_ SKILL.

## 2. Narrative & Loop Structure (form_craft)
The macro/micro open-loop architecture, escalation, and ending that hold the viewer to the payoff.

- **Start from the payoff / design backward from the send-moment** — name the ONE moment a viewer would screenshot or send to a specific friend; build backward. The most important intake field. _Why:_ anchors structure toward a sendable climax (sends = top IG reach signal); forces a real payoff so coupling doesn't break. _Src:_ SKILL, cretion.
- **Open-loop (Zeigarnik) macro + micro architecture** — one dominant macro-loop (resolves at end, never telegraphed) + micro-loops opened/closed every 5–8s; reset the macro-loop mid-video at the predicted dip. _Why:_ Zeigarnik 1927 — unfinished tasks stay active in working memory. _Src:_ SKILL, research-foundations, cretion, external-findings.
- **Micro-loops over one giant cliffhanger** — many small questions resolved in seconds beat a single huge unresolved loop. _Why:_ too many simultaneous loops overload → abandonment; rapid resolution keeps curiosity in a sustainable band (small RPE per close). _Src:_ research-foundations, SKILL, cretion.
- **Question-chain (nested overlapping loops)** — resolve micro-loop A while opening B in the same beat; macro closes only at the end. _Why:_ continuous Zeigarnik tension; each resolution a small RPE. _Src:_ SKILL, hook-structure-lib, cretion.
- **Stair-step escalation: show-don't-tell, don't telegraph, don't narrate chronologically** — each segment exceeds the last; "crazy progression" = (1) flash later peaks then reset, (2) SHOW escalating events rather than narrating chronologically; reorder for a front-loaded payoff. _Why:_ RPE (Schultz 1997/2016) — telegraphed payoffs produce nothing; picture superiority + non-linear ordering keep each beat ahead of prediction. _Src:_ SKILL, research-foundations, hook-structure-lib, cretion.
- **Short-form body arcs** — problem→agitate→reveal; list-with-withheld-best; myth→bust→proof; before/after with hidden method; one idea per beat. _Why:_ each opens a loop and withholds the payoff; one-idea-per-beat respects working memory. _Src:_ hook-structure-lib.
- **Story circle / narrative over lists** — 8-beat Harmon/Campbell circle for narrative content; deliver as story, not bullets. _Why:_ neural coupling (Hasson 2010) + narrative transportation (Green & Brock 2000) — story syncs brains and lowers counter-arguing; lists don't couple. _Src:_ hook-structure-lib, cretion.
- **Loop-count → format mapping** — 1 macro + 0–2 micro → short-form (15–90s); 1 macro + 3–8 micro → long-form (8–25min); single claim → static. State format + loop map before drafting; never pad a thin idea or cram a rich one. _Why:_ loop density predicts sustainable runtime; format follows the idea, not a target. *(Exact micro-loop boundary differs slightly across docs — treat as directional.)* _Src:_ SKILL, cretion.
- **Ending by goal metric** — loop close (last frame = first frame, 2–3× watch-time, best for completion); abrupt close on the payoff; send-trigger; save-trigger; comment-trigger; click-through; playlist chain. _Why:_ different metrics need different endings; padding sags the curve; niche specificity cues the imagined-recipient send. _Src:_ SKILL, hook-structure-lib, cretion.
- **Unified recursive blueprint** — Hook → Open Loop → Retain (interrupt + payoff) → Re-hook → Reward → Loop, recursive across short/long; Hook-Retain-Reward (Hormozi) and AIDA are coarser scaffolds. _Why:_ unifies Loewenstein + Sokolov/Mayer + Schultz RPE + Hasson. _Src:_ cretion.

## 3. Pacing & Visual Editing (form_craft)
Shot length, cut cadence, interrupt density, motion, value-per-second, and visual-referent type.

- **Pattern interrupt every 2–4s, rotate types** (the exemplar runs one every ~1.3s, 26 hard cuts in 25s) — cut/angle/B-roll/SFX/zoom/prop/action through at least the first 15s; rotate across categories. _Why:_ orienting-response reset (Sokolov); rotating fights cross-stimulus habituation. _Src:_ SKILL, research-foundations, hook-structure-lib, cretion, external-findings.
- **Fast opening ASL then let takes breathe (match to genre/funnel)** — ASL ~1.5–2.5s early (~0.8–2s for viral short-form), then longer; challenge needs relentless tempo, video-essay tolerates ASL 4–10s+. _Why:_ Cutting et al. 2011; fast pacing protects the high-risk early window; genre sets tolerable idea density. _Src:_ SKILL, research-foundations, audio-orch, cretion, audio-patterns.
- **Motivated cuts on accents; motion in every frame; animated background layer; breathe at emotional moments** — match-on-action over arbitrary beat-cuts; even a slow push-in beats a lock-off; a long take against fast pacing is itself an interrupt. _Why:_ motion = continuous exogenous capture; the long-take discrepancy re-fires the orienting response. _Src:_ audio-orch, SKILL, cretion, external-findings.
- **Sub-100ms sensory-burst climax (stills below 4 frames)** — 9 cuts in <800ms, shots <100ms near the end; use `<Img>` stills (not video) below ~4 frames to avoid decoder first-frame flash. _Why:_ rapid-fire burst spikes arousal at the climax (drives completion/sharing). _Src:_ external-findings.
- **Value-per-second audit + jump cuts** — every beat adds something new; cut beats under ~80% value-per-second; jump-cut out "ums"/dead air. _Why:_ restated/empty beats are extraneous load with no RPE. _Src:_ SKILL, cretion.
- **−30ms pre-emptive visual lead (L-cut feel)** — shift visuals ~30ms earlier than their audio (2 frames @60fps); one global offset; do NOT apply to SFX punctuation. _Why:_ mimics NLE L-cut rhythm; offsetting SFX would desync perceived cause-effect. _Src:_ external-findings.
- **Capture-time pacing prep** — shoot multiple angles (B-cam) for cheap interrupts; choreograph physical interrupts; capture a B-roll referent for every abstract/numeric claim. _Why:_ angle changes are the cheapest reliable orienting reset; picture superiority. _Src:_ cretion.
- **Convert abstract/numeric points to visuals — pick the visual TYPE by goal** — for an abstract concept, a concrete **metaphor** maximizes recall/retention/shareability, while an accurate **diagram** conveys comprehension; choose by whether the beat must be remembered/sent vs understood. _Why:_ dual-coding (Paivio 1971) + picture superiority; metaphor-vs-diagram is a retention-vs-understanding trade-off. _Src:_ SKILL, research-foundations, cretion, audio-patterns.
- **Coherence: cut decoration** — cut B-roll/music/effects/SFX that don't carry meaning, even when "fun." _Why:_ Mayer coherence — extraneous material is wasted load. *(The "23/23 confirmations, median d=0.86" effect size is single-sourced to audio-patterns; the other docs state it qualitatively.)* _Src:_ audio-patterns, research-foundations, cretion.

## 4. Captions & On-Screen Text (form_craft)
Burned-in text for muted feeds: segmentation, emphasis, kinetic state machines, placement.

- **Assume mute / burn-in captions as primary channel** — ~half of Reels watched muted; on mute, captions carry (not duplicate) content. _Why:_ muted context inverts Mayer redundancy; dual-coding for sound-on. _Src:_ SKILL, research-foundations, cretion.
- **Keyword captions, segmented & emphasized — not full transcripts** — word/short-phrase reveals (Kruger 2013), bold/color the load-bearing keyword, pop a larger keyword overlay; when voice carries complexity keep full captions de-emphasized. _Why:_ Mayer signaling + redundancy + segmentation. _Src:_ SKILL, research-foundations, hook-structure-lib, cretion, audio-patterns.
- **Title hook & text-hook placement** — 3–6 word bold title in the top third (above the thumb), naming stakes; a second under-chin text-hook that reinforces or contradicts the verbal line. _Why:_ top-third avoids thumb occlusion; semantic contradiction amplifies the gap. _Src:_ SKILL, hook-structure-lib, cretion.
- **Kinetic caption state machine & WPM-tuned grouping** — animate each word through upcoming/active/spoken states; 3–7 word phrases; set `combineTokensWithinMilliseconds = 60000/WPM` (~238ms at 252 WPM); named presets (Hormozi/MrBeast/Karaoke/Minimal). _Why:_ word-by-word reveal sustains novelty + anchors gaze; cadence-matching keeps word-sync; presets match expectations. _Src:_ external-findings.
- **Spatial/temporal contiguity** — keep related words/images close; label adjacent to referent; caption synced ~150ms lead, ≤2 lines; keyword + SFX + verbal mention within ~500ms (ideally 100ms). _Why:_ Mayer contiguity reduces extraneous load; tight co-timing feeds multisensory binding. _Src:_ cretion, audio-patterns.

## 5. Audio, Color & Multimodal Orchestration (form_craft)
The richest, most subject-independent cluster. Score-first authoring of music, SFX, voice, AND color, frame-accurately synchronized.

- **Score-first / cut-to-audio inversion; 8-track beat-sheet** — lock the audio bed + emotion-intensity curve and mark downbeats BEFORE cutting; author 8 tracks per beat: (1) music state, (2) SFX, (3) voice [WPM/pitch/emotion], (4) picture, (5) cut/motion, (6) on-screen text, (7) **color/grade shift**, (8) target emotion; fire ≥3 layers coincidentally at every accent. _Why:_ authoring all sensory layers first ensures coincident firing (superadditivity) + a coherent emotion arc. _Src:_ SKILL, audio-orch, audio-patterns.
- **Color/grade as a synchronized layer** — frame-accurate state machine, not a global look: neutral baseline; warm-saturated punch (Sat +10, 2–4 frames) fired ON the reveal inside the 100ms window; cool-desaturated during tension. _Why:_ color is the 7th coincident track; warm-punch-on-reveal contributes to superadditivity at the dopamine peak. _Src:_ audio-patterns.
- **100ms binding window: PREFER stacking many cheap micro-cues (inverse effectiveness)** — SFX + hard cut + text pop + color punch + vocal-emphasis within ~100ms fuse into one amplified hit; outside ~300ms they decouple. Because weaker elements gain proportionally more, deliberately stack small cues (2-frame text pop + faint tick + 2-frame warm punch) rather than one big effect. _Why:_ multisensory superadditivity (Stein & Meredith 1993) + inverse effectiveness (Stanford & Stein 2007). _Src:_ SKILL, research-foundations, audio-orch, audio-patterns.
- **Cut on downbeats/half-bar accents, bass-heavy beds** — cut/reveal text on accents (not every beat — that overloads); kick/808/sub beds make beat-matched cuts satisfying. _Why:_ beat entrainment (Nozaradan/Lenc 2018) + Dynamic Attending (Large) — attention peaks at expected beats. _Src:_ SKILL, research-foundations, audio-orch, audio-patterns, external-findings.
- **Frisson reveal stack (earn the drop, reserve headroom)** — pre-silence (200–400ms) → subito forte → frequency broadening (sub-bass + high treble) → new instrument/voice → fire on the visual reveal + text pop + warm punch in the 100ms window; whoosh leads the visual; sub-bass <60Hz reserved for reveals. **Reserve dynamic headroom around the reveal so the hot social master doesn't crush the subito-forte jump.** _Why:_ Huron 2006 frisson; Salimpoor & Zatorre 2011 (anticipation [caudate] vs peak [nucleus accumbens] are distinct pathways). _Src:_ SKILL, research-foundations, audio-orch, audio-patterns.
- **SFX function menu** — whoosh/sweep (orienting + bridges cuts), riser (anticipation), boom/sub-bass on reveal (frisson), tick/clock (time-pressure), UI ping (learned "new info"), vocal chop (contagion), ASMR triggers, strategic silence. SFX anchors on the visual (no −30ms lead). _Why:_ each exploits orienting response, learned codes, or contagion; silence maximizes contrast. _Src:_ audio-patterns, SKILL, cretion, external-findings.
- **Voice as load-bearing channel; prosody is the coupling mechanism; pause AFTER reveals** — narration > text-over-visuals for complex content; never monotone; whisper-then-spike on reveals; pause 200–600ms BEFORE key terms (signaling) AND AFTER reveals (let the dopamine peak land); wider pitch range = positive activation; human voice > synthetic for high-stakes. _Why:_ Mayer modality (median d=0.88); Hasson 2010 coupling (monotone breaks it); Salimpoor post-reveal peak. _Src:_ SKILL, research-foundations, audio-orch, audio-patterns.
- **Speech rate is an EDIT** — ~155 WPM raw, trim to ~180–200 (tutorial) or 200–230 (news/hype); exemplar runs ~252 WPM (hot, leaning on kinetic captions); ceiling ~215 WPM speaking, comprehension cliff ~275 WPM. _Why:_ Foulke & Sticht 1969; Beasley & Maki 1976; pace is itself a retention cue. _Src:_ SKILL, research-foundations, audio-orch, audio-patterns, external-findings.
- **Mix, EQ, loudness as retention levers** — voice ~6–8 dB above bed; side-chain duck 1.5–3 dB; high-pass <80Hz, presence 2–5kHz, de-ess 5–8kHz, noise floor <−60dBFS; master −14 LUFS canonical + hot −10/−12 LUFS social. _Why:_ protects the verbal channel; one-directional platform loudness normalization means under-mastered audio is never boosted; quiet = scrolled past. *(cretion line 256 gives absolute levels implying a wider gap — prefer the relative spec from the two audio docs.)* _Src:_ SKILL, audio-orch, audio-patterns, cretion.
- **Multi-track audio stack & beat-sync tooling** — voice + music bed + SFX/trending underlayer; beat-synced cuts via beat-to-frame math. All-matched (tempo~cut~speech) for hype only; calm-voice-over-driving-bed = authority; sparse-bed-under-fast-narration = tutorial default. _Why:_ concurrent layers raise arousal; the three strategies manage arousal vs load differently. _Src:_ external-findings, audio-orch, audio-patterns.

## 6. Platform & Distribution Signals (platform_distribution)
Per-platform ranking-signal targeting, specs, audio discovery, QA, iteration. Weights drift — treat multipliers as directional.

- **Per-platform signal priorities (goal metric decides ending/CTA)** — IG Reels: sends/reach (strongest) > watch-time > likes > audio-page; design the "send to one friend" moment. TikTok: completion + rewatch/loop > shares. YT Shorts: % watched > raw seconds. YT long-form: CTR + AVD + session watch-time together → playlists. X/LinkedIn: first line. _Why:_ platform-stated signals (Mosseri 2023–24; TikTok 2020; YouTube relative-retention). *(The "~70% completion = full TikTok distribution" figure is creator-marketing folklore — industry estimate, not a hard fact.)* _Src:_ SKILL, research-foundations, hook-structure-lib, cretion, external-findings.
- **CTR and AVD/AVP must rise together (no clickbait mismatch)** — long-form CTR target 6–10% (<4% = not pulling); APV >50% strong, <40% deprioritized; thumbnail/brand consistency lifts returning-viewer CTR. _Why:_ the algorithm reads CTR/retention mismatch as dissatisfaction. _Src:_ SKILL, hook-structure-lib, cretion.
- **Audio as discovery cluster & trending-sound lifecycle** — platforms surface to people who engaged with a sound; ride the Breakout "Golden Window" (1k–50k uses, ~Day 3–5), skip Saturation (>1M). **YouTube Shorts is the exception — original audio + voice tends to win** (record platform on the Pattern so trending audio isn't mis-applied to Shorts). Build a 15–30s signature original sound. _Why:_ algorithmic clustering by shared-sound engagement; early-rising sounds get boosts. _Src:_ SKILL, audio-orch, cretion, audio-patterns, external-findings.
- **Format & length specs** — 9:16 full-bleed, 60fps (headroom for sub-100ms bursts); short runtime (~25s) to clear completion. Clusters: TikTok 11–18s entertainment / 30–60s educational; Reels 7–15s loops / 30–45s stories; Shorts 15–35s. Relative watch-time for short, absolute for long. _Why:_ full-bleed maximizes salience; shorter runtime is mathematically easier to keep above completion threshold. _Src:_ external-findings, cretion.
- **Low-friction single-word CTA** — end with a minimal-effort ask (comment a single word). _Why:_ lower friction raises conversion; comment volume signals engagement. _Src:_ external-findings.
- **Stage-7 pre-publish QA gate (8 named pass/fail tests)** — (1) Hook test (cover first 3s); (2) TBW/100ms test (scrub every SFX-to-visual pairing); (3) Modality test (text not competing with narration); (4) Coherence test; (5) Mute test; (6) Phone-speaker test; (7) LUFS check; (8) Loop test. _Why:_ the **forward acceptance gate** that a Recreation actually instantiated the Pattern's mechanisms before shipping — complementary to retrospective diagnosis. _Src:_ audio-patterns.
- **Diagnose-from-retention-graph + numeric threshold ladder + A/B** — first cliff = hook problem; gradual slope = missing RPE; replay spikes = replicate that beat. **Advance:** short-form completion >50%, long-form APV >40% & CTR >5%; A/B win = short-form 2s hold-rate +≥15% or long-form CTR +≥1pt. **Scale:** sends/reach >1% (Reels), loop >120% (TikTok), APV >55% (YT). **Refresh:** AVD −20% WoW → refresh bed library; 3s hold-rate <50% → recut. Pivot when completion/CTR rise but sends/saves stay flat. _Why:_ the retention curve is ground truth; the ladder gives transferable accept/iterate gates. _Src:_ cretion, audio-patterns.
- **Treat specific stats as directional folklore** — plan on the mechanism, not the multiplier (8.4s watch, +32% captions, 2.3× beat-sync, ~70% completion are vendor/creator folklore; weights drift). _Src:_ SKILL, research-foundations, audio-orch, audio-patterns.

## 7. Emotional & Arousal Design (content_angle)
Choosing/engineering the target emotion and arousal arc that drive sharing. A content decision that parameterizes form.

- **Design for high arousal, not positivity; pick ONE target emotion up front** — awe/anger/anxiety/surprise/amusement; avoid low-arousal sadness/contentment unless en route to a high-arousal payoff; don't default the whole feed to one emotion. _Why:_ Berger & Milkman 2012 — physiological arousal (not valence) drives transmission. _Src:_ SKILL, research-foundations, cretion, audio-patterns.
- **Arousal-direction test for every choice** — measure every audio/visual choice against whether it raises sympathetic arousal toward awe/anger/anxiety/amusement. _Src:_ audio-patterns.
- **Emotion arc as an intensity curve** — sketch a 0–1 curve (spike → oscillate → 0.95 payoff/frisson → resolve → final loop spike); oscillating arousal re-engages and reserves the top peak for the payoff. _Src:_ audio-patterns.
- **Tempo→arousal (non-monotonic), mode→valence** — fast >120 BPM = highest arousal; **mid ~100–110 BPM (esp. ~106, matching resting HR) is paradoxically the LOWEST-arousal band** — chosen for tutorials so arousal doesn't compete with learning; slow <76 BPM = contemplative (more arousing than mid). Major = hope, minor = dread; minor→major modulation manufactures relief on the payoff. _Why:_ tempo psychophysics (Wang 2025 EEG: 106 BPM lowest arousal); mode→valence. _Src:_ audio-orch, audio-patterns.

## 8. Content Angle & Subject Strategy (content_angle)
Topical framing, genre archetype, shot mix, persuasion, funnel position. The least transferable layer.

- **Abstract angle moves (transfer as strategies)** — contrarian correction, myth-bust, before/after-with-hidden-method, problem-agitate-reveal, stakes-up-front, "no dull moments"/wow-factor. Substance/utility compounds; gimmicks decay. _Why:_ each pairs a gap with an arousal trigger; awe + utility outlasts spectacle (Berger & Milkman: usefulness is an independent share predictor). _Src:_ hook-structure-lib, SKILL, research-foundations, cretion.
- **Narrative transportation (lower resistance)** — wrap persuasion in an absorbing narrative to reduce counter-arguing. *(Logged as a strategy here, a mechanism under the substrate, and underlying the story-circle form tactic — one effect, linked across three facets.)* _Why:_ Green & Brock 2000. _Src:_ research-foundations, cretion.
- **Genre/content-archetype binding (content-specific)** — classify by archetype (comedy reel / ASMR vlog / explainer / model launch / breaking news / tutorial) and apply its recipe; balance shot types. _Why:_ formats follow distinct templates; shot-mix & emotion presets are tuned to the subject genre. *(The "40% talking-head / 40% UI-code / 20% B-roll" engineering mix comes from external-findings, NOT the audio docs.)* _Src:_ external-findings, hermes, audio-patterns, audio-orch.
- **Funnel position & audience-overlap as a strategic axis** — hype/awe = TOP-of-funnel (reach: sends, CTR, broad distribution); video-essay/calm-trust = LOWER-funnel (session depth, committed audience, audience-overlap). This flows from the subject and dictates which goal metric and ending the form facets target. _Why:_ the MrBeast-vs-essay continuum determines the reach-vs-depth signal a Pattern can realistically win — match optimization to the borrowed style's funnel position. _Src:_ cretion, audio-patterns.
- **Authority / social-proof surrogates & human presence** — slide-in notification banners (iMessage/WhatsApp) as the social-proof slot; circular face-cam PiP so a human face persists over UI/B-roll; vision-verified B-roll relevance (VLM match >~0.6). _Why:_ familiar messaging UI implies third-party validation; a visible face is a strong involuntary pull + parasocial connection. _Src:_ external-findings.

## 9. Exit-Cost & Attention Substrate (psychology_substrate) — the WHY
Not a tactic layer; the explanatory premises the form tactics serve. Linked from the tactics each powers.

- **Cost-of-exit, not attention capacity (audiences also binge for hours)** — retention is continuous re-justification against a one-thumb-flick exit; minimize perceived exit incentive each micro-moment. The positive corollary licenses long-form depth: humans sustain attention for hours when held. _Why:_ Gloria Mark's data shows reduced task-switching, not a sustained-attention ceiling. _Powers:_ value-per-second, interrupt cadence, loudness-as-retention, loop-count→format.
- **Target playback environment = tinny phone speaker, frequently muted, one-handed** — the unifying constraint behind scattered rules: WHY bright/rough timbre, WHY noise floor <−60dBFS is fatal, WHY loudness is a retention lever, WHY captions are primary, WHY the mute/phone-speaker QA tests exist. _Powers:_ timbre choice, noise-floor spec, hot LUFS master, burn-in captions, top-third placement, Stage-7 tests.
- **Orienting response & its habituation** — Sokolov 1963; novelty reflex habituates near-exponentially, re-fires on discrepancy. _Powers:_ hook stack, interrupts, rotate-don't-repeat, re-hooks, motion-in-every-frame, whoosh/silence.
- **Priority-map salience capture** — Theeuwes/Gonthier; Yantis & Jonides 1990; faces/motion/onsets/contrast capture bottom-up. _Powers:_ first-frame salience, thumbnails, full-bleed, brand consistency.
- **Information-gap curiosity** — Loewenstein 1994; Kang 2009 fMRI; small specific gaps fire, vague ones suppress. _Powers:_ write-hook-first, calibrated gap, hook-type library, title/thumbnail gap.
- **Zeigarnik open-loop tension** — unfinished tasks stay active; too many loops overload → abandonment. _Powers:_ macro/micro loops, question chain, mid-video reset, withheld-best lists.
- **Reward prediction error & variable schedule** — Schultz 1997/2016; Salimpoor & Zatorre 2011; better-than-expected spikes, telegraphed yields nothing; the build is itself rewarding; pause AFTER the reveal lets the peak land. _Powers:_ stair-step escalation, show-don't-tell, don't-telegraph, micro-loop resolutions, frisson build, pause-after-reveal, slope diagnosis.
- **Cognitive load (Sweller/Mayer)** — two limited channels; intrinsic/extraneous/germane. _Powers:_ one-idea-per-beat, coherence, signaling, modality (d=0.88), redundancy, segmenting, contiguity, metaphor-vs-diagram.
- **Neural coupling & narrative transportation** — Hasson 2010; Green & Brock 2000; story couples brains, lists don't; flat prosody breaks coupling. _Powers:_ story circle, narrative-over-lists, transportation angle, prosodic variation, human-voice-for-high-stakes.
- **Multisensory superadditivity & binding window** — Stein & Meredith 1993; ~100–250ms window; weaker elements gain proportionally more; sound pre-primes vision. _Powers:_ 100ms rule, stack-coincident-layers, prefer-cheap-micro-cues, frisson stack, whoosh-leads-reveal, beat-entrainment cuts.
- **Frisson acoustics & arousal-driven sharing** — Huron 2006/Grewe 2007 (chills in 30–50%, sub-threshold arousal in most); Berger & Milkman 2012. _Powers:_ frisson stack, whisper-then-spike, tempo/mode mapping, emotion-arc, target-emotion selection.
- **Ethics / habituation ceiling** — audiences habituate to escalation (fresh substance is the durable edge); the variable-reward loop is the one implicated in compulsive use. Write content you'd be comfortable having someone you care about consume on repeat. _Src:_ SKILL, cretion.

## Cross-cutting operating principles

- **Form and content are separable but linked.** Store both; form facets are the durable transferable core; content facets carry a *rebind-on-Recreation* expectation. Content (target emotion, payoff/send-moment, genre, funnel position) *parameterizes* form via the substrate.
- **Verify forward AND diagnose backward.** Run the Stage-7 QA gate before publishing (acceptance test that the Pattern landed); then read the retention graph against the numeric ladder to decide advance/scale/refresh.
- **Mechanism over multiplier.** Cited multipliers are folklore; the mechanisms (orienting response, curiosity gap, Zeigarnik, RPE, cognitive load, neural coupling, superadditivity + inverse effectiveness, frisson, arousal-driven sharing) are the reusable truth.
- **The bottleneck is cost-of-exit, not attention capacity** — but audiences binge for hours when held. Every beat must re-earn the next.
