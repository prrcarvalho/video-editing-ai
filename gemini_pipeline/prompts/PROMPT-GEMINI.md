  # THE FRAMEWORK (apply, don't restate)
  Numbers in this section are typical reference values from research, given so you can compare —
  they are NOT targets to find in the video and NOT boundaries for your analysis.
  1. EXIT COST: viewers leave when swiping is cheaper than staying. Find the moments where the video
     "out-bids the swipe" and any moments where it fails to.
  2. ORIENTING RESPONSE: attention is re-fired by pattern interrupts (cut, zoom, angle change, SFX,
     prop, new text, semantic surprise) and fades quickly between them. Rotating interrupt TYPES
     matters more than raw frequency — a repeated identical interrupt stops working.
  3. CURIOSITY LOOPS: small, specific knowledge gaps create curiosity; vague big ones don't.
     Retention typically comes from a chain of short micro-loops under one macro-loop that closes at
     the end. A loop that closes too early or never closes is a leak.
  4. PREDICTION ERROR: the payoff must EXCEED what the hook implicitly promised. A telegraphed or
     merely-adequate payoff causes drop-off at the reveal.
  5. AROUSAL → SHARING: high-arousal emotions (awe, amusement, anger, anxiety) drive sends/shares;
     low-arousal ones (contentment, mild interest) don't. Awe + practical utility beats spectacle.
  6. AUDIOVISUAL SYNC: an SFX, cut, and text-pop amplify each other only when they land essentially
     together (research puts the fusion window around a tenth of a second); clearly separated events
     decouple and do nothing for each other. Small synced micro-events punch above their weight.
     Cuts landing on musical downbeats hit attentional peaks; off-beat cuts land in troughs.
  7. FRISSON STACK (anatomy of a strong payoff): a brief silence or drop-out → sudden loudness jump →
     frequency broadening (sub-bass plus high sparkle) → a new sound entering → all fired together on
     the visual reveal. Report which of these elements are present and which are absent.
  8. CAPTIONS & COGNITIVE LOAD: a large share of viewers watch muted, so on-screen text is a primary
     channel. The strong form is keyword captions, revealed word-by-word, with load-bearing words
     signaled (color/scale). Full verbatim transcripts and decorative elements that carry no meaning
     are wasted load. Text that contradicts the spoken line creates productive tension.
  9. VOICE: silence-trimmed, energetic delivery retains; flat monotone kills engagement. A pause
     placed just BEFORE a key term makes it land harder than a pause after it.
  10. ENDING: strong videos stop ON the payoff with no outro. A loop sandwich (last frame matches the
      first frame) drives seamless rewatches. The strongest Instagram reach signal is sends: the best
      endings make the payoff so specific the viewer pictures one particular friend to send it to.
